take me back to my past!

symbiosis with our new machine gods

most software manufactured by a large corporation seems to just be poorly designed and awful. you can really taste the lack of passion and desire for a paycheck in a lot of mainstream software design today. maybe its just incompetence – i dont know. the point is shit sucks dude. and with all this faux ai shit being shoved down our throats i cant help but feel that these people are actively working against us

take microsoft's windows 11 for instance: the settings are an absolute mess of things that i definitely dont want to do and will likely never plan to! things like displaying seconds in your system clock are a GARGANTUAN task. go ahead! try to figure it out without looking stuff up. these people* didnt have you, the USER, in mind while designing the USER interface. seriously, who's fucking up this bad here?

*if you think im being judgemental here, i am. i feel as though we as the little guys reserve a right to judge our tech overlords at least a little.

i really do lose it a lot of the time when i use software designed by big tech. another anecdote includes my smart tv that my girlfriend and i got for our apartment.
...
the thing BLOWS! it takes so much cognitive effort to use and it only really gets in your way! just today, i was trying to open the damn thing to watch some slop and it wouldnt stop playing a live broadcast of god-knows-what-team's football game.

shut the hell up! i dont wanna watch football!
...
it seems like every time i want to actually engage with a platform one of these big tech companies offer, i have to sacrifice something in some way. that's why ive moved away from (para)social media. i want to use my computer and not have to give up my time or energy in an unfulfilling way.

though this is the case with most software, i think a more do-it-yourself approach to computers can make them less of a burden on my life and more of a machinal extension of myself. i started that today by setting up a pi-hole. what it does boils down to blocking trackers and ads on a network-wide scale. i originally did it because i didnt want ads on youtube on my smart tv in addition to not having tim apple's nose all up in my business all the time.

additional things ive thought about are setting up my own sort of music playing system using just a raspberry pi, a screen, and some audio files (obviously a speaker and all the good audio stuff too but whatever).
...
i want to start treating computers not as devices for stimulation laden with advertisements and predatory business practices, as they are coming to be – but as an extension and advancement of the things people were already doing: listening to music, playing games, doing some work, being creative, etc.

as for the whole music making side of things, i love hardware! i think interfacing something with buttons and optionally some other modulation media with a computer-sound-producer aids in the creative process greatly. i find that i hate looking at a screen after a while because its very easy for my primary ego reference to dissolve behind the screen.

i hope in the coming months to do more weird computer shit while also... using...... computers............ less?
oxymoronic, i know, but you get the idea.
